Norhan
                 Addressed to a broad audience, The Morals of History offers a wide-ranging study 
                  of the role of value judgements in culture, as Todorov explores the relationship
                   between facts and values, truth and fiction, interpretation and articulation.
                    Todorov studies a variety of travel narratives from those 
                  of Columbus to Amerigo Vespucci to Lamartine, and he analyzes with great clarity 
                  the writings of the  "ideologues" of both colonialism and anticolonialism.
